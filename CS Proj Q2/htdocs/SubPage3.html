Climate Change causes warmer temperatures in water, which can harm certain marine organisms that are sensitive to changes in temperature. Rising carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere also make ocean water more acidic, killing coral reefs which are crucial to keeping the oceanâ€™s ecosystems healthy.

Rainfall is more likely to happen more frequently and more intensely as a result of climate change, and there may be more flooding incidents as a result. Reduced groundwater supply for drinking water in some areas may also have an impact on groundwater recharge.

Energy production, infrastructure, human health, agriculture, and ecosystems are just a few of the industries that will be impacted by climate change's effects on water availability and quality.
